Ask ICC

WHY DO I NEED TO BE BAPTIZED?

Baptism symbolizes conversion to Jesus and should follow a confession of faith in Christ. It is your public and unashamed confession that you are leaving your old way of life to follow Him.

Throughout the New Testament, We see Jesus, the apostles, and the early church practicing baptism.

When someone would repent of their sins and turn to Jesus they would then immediately be baptized as an outward sign of the inward change that Jesus had done in their hearts.

When you step out of the water, you are letting the world know that you have walked into a new life of faith in Jesus Christ and that you are committing to follow Jesus down a new path. Matthew 28:19, Acts 2:38, 8:35-38, Romans 6:1-4

WHAT SHOULD I EXPECT?

Before you attend, we want to make sure that you know you are welcome at Ishaka community Church.

You can expect a place that continually challenges you to grow in your relationship with Christ, connect with others, and to live on mission to reach others.

You can expect the service to last a little over an hour.

We do our best to create an environment every week where people from different backgrounds, different cultures, and different faith traditions can come and discover a life in Jesus Christ. There is no pressure and no judgment; only an exciting expectation to connect with God when arriving for a service.

You can expect a relaxed and judgement-free atmosphere where you can come just as you are.

You can expect a church where your entire family (babies, kids, teenagers, college students, and adults) can find a place to connect and grow.

You can expect to be welcomed in the lobby with coffee, bagels, snacks, etc. and people stationed to help guide you to the right place.
